Manasi Sharma

Hi there! I am a second year MS student in Computer Science at Stanford University on the Artificial Intelligence track. I am currently member of the Stanford Vision Lab under Prof. Fei-Fei Li and Prof. Jiajun Wu, where I work on the BEHAVIOR project in robotic simulation benchmarking and the AIDA (Attention-driven data augmentation) project which aims to improve image classification performance using a saliency-map based augmentation. I consider myself a generalist in AI and am interested in the broad domains of Deep Learning, Computer Vision, Explainability, Reinforcement Learning & Decision Making, Graph Neural Networks.

I graduated in 2021 from Columbia University with a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Physics. As an undergrad, I was supervised by Prof. Daniel Hsu and Prof. Zoltan Haiman on interpreting astrophysical deep learning models for weak lensing using an array of saliency map methods. I started off my undergraduate program with a broad interest in computational physics and first pursued the direction of AI at Caltech in 2019, under Prof. Mansi Kasliwal in which I developed a real/bogus image astrophysical source classifier for the IR-Gattini telescope that performed so well that it was permanently included in the telescope's data processing pipeline.

Selected Projects

For a complete list of projects, please refer to my CV.

Bottleneck in Homogeneous Isotropic turbulence

Utilized hyperviscosity in modified Navier Stokes equations and performed DNS upto the point of near thermalization of scales in 3D turbulence. Compared different time stepping methods and their implementation for high coefficients of hyperviscosity
3D Forcing in Stratified flows

Modeled turbulence statistics in a externally forced spectra in wavenumber space, for homogeneous turbulence. Validated LES simulations for isotropic Ornstein - Uhlenbeck and anisotropic linear forcing models in a cubic isotropic turbulence condition following Eswaran et. al. on unstratified flows.

Viscous Faraday Wave Modeling

In Faraday instability, standing waves form on a vibrated liquid layer, when subjected to vertical oscillation. I simulated using a parallel periodic symmetric code, BLUE, secondary shear effect of Faraday waves, as observed in the experimental work, presented in the paper for multiple resolutions and domain sizes for a silicone oil air interface.

Analysis of Hurricane IRMA

Applied Variable Time Averaging, to deduce dominant structure period and to separate the large scale and local scale motions. Reported Non Ergodic nature of pressure fluctuations during the hurricane.

Effect of Roughness : Wind profile Models

Conventional log law profile overestimates the height and roughness dependence of atmospheric flows. A Universal profile is provided, taking into account the rapid fluctuations about the mean for different kinds of terrains using LIDAR measurements.
